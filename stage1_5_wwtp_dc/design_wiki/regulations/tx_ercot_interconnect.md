# Texas / ERCOT interconnection for a BTM AI factory

**Short version:** if your new AI load stays behind the meter and under specific export / import thresholds, ERCOT does **not** require you to enter the standard interconnection queue. Conventional grid-scale projects wait **3–7 years** in the ERCOT queue; BTM can energize in **6–12 months**.

## Key thresholds (ERCOT Protocols 2024)

* **Small Generator / 20 MW rule:** PV + BESS combined nameplate under 20 MW and not exporting to grid → no formal generator interconnection; only a facility-level Distribution Service Agreement with the local utility (Oncor, Centerpoint, AEP TX).
* **New load / 5 MW rule:** new retail load under 5 MW at an existing metered service → typically permitted as a service upgrade (transformer + switchgear) with Level 2 planning study, not an interconnection study.
* **Between 5–20 MW:** utility-specific; typically a Level 3 study ($50k–$150k, 6–9 months).

## What the WWTP + PV + DC arrangement looks like

The WWTP already has an industrial service (usually at 12.47 kV or 25 kV primary distribution). Adding:

* 5–8 MWp PV BTM: below the 20 MW export threshold if the interconnection agreement is **non-export** (PV curtails before pushing to grid).
* 4 MW DC load: if total retail load (WWTP + DC) <20 MW, stays in "service upgrade" territory.
* 8 MWh BESS: no separate interconnection if BESS sits behind the same meter.

Result: a project that avoids the ERCOT long queue entirely. **This is the core value proposition of the WWTP site.**

## What can still delay you

* Transformer upgrade at the utility substation: usually 6–9 months lead time, sometimes 18 months if the utility is capacity-constrained.
* Environmental Assessment + TCEQ construction notice: ~90 days.
* Property easement negotiation if PV needs land outside the original WWTP parcel: weeks to years.
