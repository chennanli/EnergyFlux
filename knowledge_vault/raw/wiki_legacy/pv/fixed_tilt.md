# Fixed-tilt ground mount

**The simplest PV configuration.** Modules are mounted on fixed steel frames angled at the site's latitude (give or take). No moving parts.

## Key numbers

* Land density: **~290 kWp DC per acre** at GCR 0.40.
* Specific yield: reference baseline; all other tracking options report as ratios relative to this.
* CAPEX: the cheapest per-watt installed configuration. Often **$0.80–0.95/W DC** turnkey.
* Reliability: the highest — literally no moving parts — 25-year design life, <0.5% annual degradation.

## When it wins

* Narrow, irregular, or steeply sloped parcels where tracker rows don't fit.
* Rooftop applications (always fixed).
* Very small projects (<500 kWp) where tracker CAPEX doesn't amortize.
* Sites where O&M access is expensive — remote, hard-to-reach installs.

## When it loses

* Open, flat parcels >1 MW in the continental US — single-axis tracking beats it on LCOE.

## Why show it in Blog 2 at all

For the "just starting out, is this even worth planning?" decision, fixed tilt gives a conservative floor on expected energy yield. A site that pencils out on fixed-tilt numbers will always pencil out on tracker numbers — so it's the safe anchor for a feasibility narrative.
