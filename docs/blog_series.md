# EnergyFlux Blog Series Plan

Series name: **Distributed AI Factory**

Umbrella thesis: Existing industrial sites can host behind-the-meter AI infrastructure
across a range of scales. WWTPs are one especially interesting case because they combine
land, water, electrical service, industrial zoning, and 24/7 operations in one parcel.

---

## Post 2 — The Engineering (PUBLISHED)

**Title:** How I Simulated a Wastewater Plant + Solar + AI Data Center (And What I Got Wrong)

**Goal:** Explain the repo and engineering workflow. Honest about what was modeled vs simplified.

**Status:** Published / ready to publish
**File:** `blog/blog_post_2.html`

**Must include:**
- PV modeling (pvlib, real weather)
- WWTP load model: QSDsan BSM1 steady-state solve calibrates the baseline KLa, which
  then drives an 8,760-hour diurnal profile (American municipal flow pattern, EQ-tank
  dampening). Honest about the two-stage structure.
- Asymmetric BESS dispatch logic
- DC thermal ODE
- Energy balance verification
- What would need validation at a real site

**Key honest statement (required):**
> The WWTP load comes from a hybrid model: a QSDsan BSM1 steady-state simulation
> calibrates the baseline KLa (the aeration controller output), and an 8,760-hour
> diurnal profile is then generated by scaling that BSM1-derived baseline with
> flow-pattern forcing and equalization-tank dampening. The CSV output carries
> `generation_method = QSDsan-BSM1`. This is not the same as a full-year hour-by-hour
> BSM1 ODE integration — doing that would require influent forcing files and a
> coupled dispatch loop, which is listed as future work in the roadmap.

**Title:** Which Industrial Sites Are Actually Promising, and Why Design Matters More Than Clever Control

**Goal:** Broaden beyond WWTP. Show engineering judgment about site selection and design.

**Status:** Not yet written
**File:** `blog/blog_post_3.html` (current version is a preliminary draft — needs rewrite)

**Must include:**
- Site-class comparison matrix (from `docs/site_classes.md`)
- WWTP, chemical plant, refinery/large industrial campus, and weaker alternatives
- Design sensitivity examples from WWTP case:
  - Single-axis tracker vs fixed tilt (yield difference)
  - Bifacial vs monofacial (yield difference)
  - Asymmetric BESS sizing (why 1 MW charge / 2 MW discharge)
  - Transformer headroom constraint
- The argument: good design decisions move results more than later software optimization

**Positioning:**
- WWTP is not the answer for every scenario
- Design choices at the MW-scale often matter more than control sophistication
- Different site classes suit different owner types (municipal vs private industrial)
