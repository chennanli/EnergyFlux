NVIDIA Blackwell GB200 NVL72

The first NVIDIA rack-scale Blackwell platform to ship in volume (Q3 2025). 72 B200 GPUs, NVLink switch fabric, liquid-cooled. Used as the default AI rack platform in EnergyFlux Blog 2 worked-case sizing.

Headline numbers

  • Per-rack facility draw: ~125 kW (NVIDIA spec: 120 kW typical, 132 kW peak; design to 125 kW as rated facility load).
  • Per-rack FP8 compute: ~1.4 EFLOPS sustained.
  • Inference throughput: ~5.8 × 10⁶ tokens/s per MW for 70B-parameter dense models, batch 32, FP8.
  • Cooling: liquid (rear-door HX or direct-to-chip). Facility PUE 1.15–1.25.
  • Memory: 192 GB HBM3e per GPU → 13.8 TB HBM per rack — sufficient to keep a 670B-class MoE model resident.

Why this is the default for design-stage sizing

The platform has been shipping in volume long enough that operator data is available. Per-kW thermal curves, networking constraints, and at-scale inference throughput are all observable. Design-stage estimates carry roughly <5% uncertainty.

For the Blog 2 worked case (<a class="wikilink" href="../entities/wwtp_45mgd_archetype.html">wwtp_45mgd_archetype</a>, 45 MGD, 2.62 MW DC), the rack count is straightforward: 2,620 kW / 125 kW per rack ≈ 21 racks. The earlier stage 1.5 simulation framed the same calculation around a 30 MGD / 2 MW DC point and arrived at 16 racks; that earlier number is preserved in <a class="wikilink" href="../sources/src_legacy_blackwell_gb200_nvl72.html">src_legacy_blackwell_gb200_nvl72</a> for traceability and is not the headline figure for the published Blog 2 worked case.

When to choose something else

  • Vera Rubin NVL144 (production for H2 2026): advertised 3× tokens/s-per-kW. Reconsider after 2027 when field data exists; for now, GB200 is the lower-risk specification.
  • AMD MI300X (or successors): plausible alternative at the chip level but rack-scale equivalents to NVL72 are not yet shipping with comparable interconnect.
  • Cerebras WSE-3 / wafer-scale: niche when the workload is steady-state inference on a fixed transformer architecture; not a general-purpose substitute.

Open questions for review

  • The 5.8 × 10⁶ tokens/s/MW inference number is for a specific model class and batch size; per-watt throughput on MoE models or longer-context workloads will differ.
  • Facility PUE 1.15–1.25 assumes liquid cooling and a benign ambient regime. Hot-climate WWTP sites in TX / AZ may push PUE higher.
